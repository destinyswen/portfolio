Childhood

I was raised by a immigrant family who came from West Africa, Liberia. My parents decided to move to the United States in 1998 and they decided to live in Brooklyn, New York where I ended up being born. As my early childhood years, my parents decided that it would be the best to move to Minnesota instead of staying in New York.

As a child, I fell in love with anything entertainment related. I was obsessed with anything Disney channel, obessed with the movies me and my father would watch from Blockbusters every week and I was even obsessed with the movie posters my father would also get me for my room. Something about the media playing on the TV always made me glued to the screen.

Teenage Years

As I was getting into my early teen years, the strict African roles become stronger on top of me as I became the oldest sibling to my brothers and sisters and to I’ve always had ran to certain types of media to help me escape from those extreme roles put against me and I wanted to start expressing myself by creating my own type pieces of artwork. The only problem was that I only went to white predominated schools and the media that I was really interested in was made for and by more western media and that was the only things I mimic in my artwork.

I started creating drawings, writing my own music, writting my own scripts and created my own YouTube channel with all of my fan fiction stories. I was just inspired by everything that I grew up watching and I wanted to be just like the things I saw on the screen.

Adulthood

As I got older, I notice there was a certain shift in western media when it came to diversity and inclusion. I started seeing more stories, music and artwork being created by people of all types of backgrounds and the recongition people were giving them and it inspired me to change the way I think about creating art. I overall got tired of writing stories solely based white backgrounds and wanted to start expressing my true self when it came to my artwork.

I decided that I after high school, I wanted to go to school for enhance my technique and take designing more seriously. I wanted to create more artwork that can reach other people who has lived the same experiences as me and understand the core reason why I am even so passionate about creating things. I believe that art is the only way that can understand and express the basics of the human experiences and it has help me connect to people in all different types of backgrounds and walks of life and I would love to continue expressing my passion and creating things made for people like me.

For my project, I decided that I wanted to focus on sexual health, especially towards men. The reason why I decided to make my target audience men is because for years, there was been a stigma that if a male got contacted with an STI, it’s usually their female partners fault or there is more judgement towards women for sexual health and making sure that they are informed with anything but a lot of people in my class asked, what about men? Why aren’t men getting this much pressure like they are for women, so I decided that I wanted that to be the whole focus. For my first phase of the project, I create my empathy maps and they were based on different types of men and them being in sexual situations.

These are my personas inspired by my empathy maps. Based on all of the research that I did to listen to real men stories experiencing questioning and confusion situations when it comes to sex, I wanted to make sure my personas felt authentic and like a real life situations that men could experience.

For my branding process, I decided that I wanted my project to have a unique look when focusing on sexual wellness. I decided to create an “out of this world“ theme experience since in our society, sex is still such a taboo subject and it hard for men to express themselves when they need help sexually. I decided to use more abstract space imagery to help get the point across on making men feel like they are on a new world to explore sexuality. This will also help them to get more comfortable to get educated when it comes to get tested for STIU/STD’S, Sexual Dysfunction and aftercare and not feel any judgement, a app where they to feel like they are in their own space to feel free.
